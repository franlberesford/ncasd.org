NCASD Lab Helps Launch New Project on Adolescent Substance Use

The Dynamic Network and Adolescent Substance Use project is a collaborative, interdisciplinary NIH funded effort by researchers at the University of California-Irvine to better understand the dynamics of adolescent social networks and consequences for their cigarette smoking, marijuana, and alcohol use behavior. This project is lead by Principal Investigator Cynthia Lakon, Assistant Professor, Program in Public Health, in collaboration with three co-investigators also from UC Irvine: Carter Butts, Departments of Sociology and Statistics, John Hipp, Departments of Criminology Law and Society and Sociology, and David Timberlake, Program in Public Health. Through a combination of data modeling of network tie formation, social support processes, and adolescent characteristics, and a novel simulation model assessing the consequences of these dynamic networks for adolescents’ use of cigarettes, marijuana, and alcohol use, the project team aims to understand how various aspects of adolescents’ network ties and substance use behavior might diffuse throughout networks. This project launches the Social Networks and Health Initiative at the Center for Networks and Relational Analysis, which is part of the California Institute for Telecommunications and Information Technology (Calit2).
